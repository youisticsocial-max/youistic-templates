Legal FAQ

Service Law Queries

Basic procedural details for government employees contemplating administrative challenges.

1. Who can file a case in the Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT)?

Under the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, civilian staff of the Central Government, Railway employees, Postal personnel, and civilian staff of the Defense Ministry can file original applications (OAs) before the CAT regarding service disputes.

2. Can a government employee challenge a suspension order in Court?

Yes. If a suspension order is arbitrary, exceeds statutory limits, is passed by an incompetent authority, or is not reviewed within the mandated timeline (often 90 days), it can be challenged before the High Court or CAT.

3. What is the remedy for promotion supersession?

If an employee is bypassed for promotion without proper reason (anomalous DPC, incorrect APAR grading review, etc.), they can contest the seniority list or DPC minutes before the Service Tribunal or High Court.

4. Where do Rajasthan State Government employees appeal service matters?

State employees (teachers, police, revenue staff, etc.) appeal disciplinary or service disputes before the Rajasthan Civil Services Appellate Tribunal. Writ petitions under Article 226/227 are filed in the High Court.
